# Future Improvements: Gordon Finance Engine

To evolve the Gordon Finance Engine into a world-class actuarial assistant, the following enhancements are suggested:

### 1. Fixed vs. Variable Cost Decomposition
Distinguish between non-discretionary spending (Rent, Bond, Insurance, Subscriptions) and discretionary spending (Dining, Luxury, Impulse).
*   **Goal:** Calculate a "Survival Runway"—how long you can last if you cut all non-essential spending.

### 2. Seasonality & Periodicity Analysis
Incorporate Year-over-Year (YoY) comparisons to account for seasonal spikes (e.g., December holidays, annual school fees, or quarterly taxes).
*   **Goal:** Proactive alerts for upcoming "high-spend" months based on historical patterns.

### 3. "Fat Tail" & Black Swan Risk Modeling
Move beyond Normal Distribution (Bell Curves) for risk. Financial shocks often come from rare, large-magnitude events.
*   **Goal:** Use Student's t-distributions in Monte Carlo simulations to better account for the probability of catastrophic balance-depleting events.

### 4. Machine Learning Categorisation
Utilise the AI Brain (Ollama) to perform semantic categorisation of messy bank descriptions.
*   **Goal:** Group transactions like "CHECKERS RIVONIA" and "CHECKERS CPT" into a single "Groceries" bucket with 100% accuracy, bypassing unreliable Regex.

### 5. Cash Flow & Income Projection
Integrate expected income dates (Salary, Dividends) into the timeline.
*   **Goal:** A dynamic runway that shows "gap days" between now and the next expected deposit, rather than just a static countdown.
