Built on Investec Programmable Banking

Gordon uses Investec's Open Banking API to do things no ordinary budgeting app can. Real account data, real-time — not screen scraping, not manual imports.

01

Real-time transaction sync

Every 60 seconds, Gordon fetches new transactions via the Investec Transactions API and deduplicates them into TimescaleDB.

02

Multi-account balance monitoring

Gordon aggregates balances across all your Investec accounts — cheque, savings, and money market — for an accurate net position.

03

Automated runway top-ups

When your runway drops below your threshold, Gordon can automatically trigger an inter-account transfer to keep you liquid.

04

Bank-grade security

OAuth 2.0 authentication. Your Investec credentials are never stored — only short-lived access tokens, AES-256 encrypted at rest.

FAQ

Questions, answered

Yes. Gordon connects to any personal or business account accessible via Investec's Programmable Banking API — including Private Bank, Business Current, Savings, and Money Market accounts. You authenticate via OAuth 2.0, so Gordon never sees your username or password.
Your data is encrypted at rest with AES-256 and in transit with TLS 1.3. Gordon stores transaction data in a private TimescaleDB instance — either yours (self-hosted) or ours (cloud). We never share or sell your data. On the Pro cloud plan, data is stored in South African data centres. You can request a full data export or deletion at any time.
Gordon supports Google Gemini, Anthropic Claude, OpenAI, and Ollama (local/private). You provide your own API keys, meaning your conversations with Gordon are never processed by us — they go directly from your server to the AI provider. Starter users can run Ollama locally for completely private, free AI.
Yes — on Pro and Business plans, Gordon's Runway Top-Up feature can automatically transfer funds between your Investec accounts when your runway drops below a configurable threshold. This uses the Investec Transfers API and requires explicit setup and consent in your settings. It is disabled by default and every transfer is logged.
